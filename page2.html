Leche Flan

Ingredients


    For the caramel:

  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water

    • For the custard:

  • 10 large egg yolks
  • 1 can (390g) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) whole milk (optional, for a smoother texture)

Instructions

  • In a medium saucepan over low to medium heat, combine the granulated sugar and water. Stir until the sugar dissolves. Once dissolved, stop stirring and let it cook undisturbed for about 8-10 minutes, or until the syrup turns golden brown. Be careful not to burn the sugar.
  • Immediately pour the caramel into your flan molds (lumped aluminum molds or a baking dish). Swirl the molds to coat the bottom evenly with the caramel. Let it cool and harden.
  • In a large mixing bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth and slightly frothy. Add the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, whole milk (if using), and vanilla extract to the egg yolks. Whisk everything together until fully combined and smooth. Strain the custard mixture through a fine sieve into another bowl or measuring cup to remove any lumps or bubbles. This step will ensure your flan is smooth and velvety.
  • Carefully pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the molds, filling them almost to the top. Be gentle to avoid disturbing the caramel layer.
  • Prepare a steamer or use a large pot with a lid. Bring water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low to maintain a gentle simmer. Arrange the molds in the steamer, making sure they do not touch the water. You can place a clean cloth or paper towel under the lid to catch any condensation and prevent it from dripping onto the flan. Steam for 45-60 minutes, or until the flan is set. To check, insert a toothpick into the center of the flan—if it comes out clean, the flan is done.
  • Tip: Check the water level periodically while steaming, adding more hot water if necessary to prevent it from drying out.
  • Once done, remove the molds from the steamer and let them cool to room temperature. Refrigerate the leche flan for at least 2 hours or until fully chilled.
  • To serve, run a knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it from the mold. Invert the mold onto a serving plate. The caramel will flow over the flan, creating a glossy sauce. Slice and enjoy!
