Glazed Donut

Ingredients


For the donuts:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) warm milk (about 110°F or 45°C)
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 1/2 cups (440g) all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) warm water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

    • For the glaze:

  • 2 cups (240g) powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk (or water)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  • In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Stir until the sugar dissolves and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy and bubbly. This means the yeast is active.

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the yeast mixture with melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Stir until the mixture is well combined. Gradually add the flour, 1 cup at a time, along with the salt, mixing after each addition until the dough starts to come together.
  • Once the dough begins to form, turn it out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  • Alternatively, you can use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment to knead the dough for about 5 minutes on low speed.
  • Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  • Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface. Roll the dough out to about 1/2 inch (1.25 cm) thickness.

  • Use a round cutter (about 3 inches or 7.5 cm) to cut out the donut shapes. For the donut holes, use a smaller cutter or a bottle cap. Gather the excess dough, roll it out again, and continue cutting until all the dough is used up.
  • Heat about 2-3 inches of vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  • Carefully drop the donut rings and holes into the hot oil, a few at a time, and fry for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown.

  • Use a slotted spoon to remove the donuts from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  • While the donuts are cooling, make the glaze by whisking together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract in a bowl until smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more milk for a thinner glaze or more powdered sugar for a thicker glaze.
  • Once the donuts have cooled slightly, dip them into the glaze while they’re still warm. Allow the glaze to set for a few minutes, then serve.
