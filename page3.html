Brownies

Ingredients


  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 1/4 cups (250g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (50g) packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (45g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder (optional for slightly fluffier brownies)
  • 1/2 cup (90g) chocolate chips (optional, for extra richness)

Instructions

  • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • Grease an 8x8-inch (20x20 cm) baking pan or line it with parchment paper for easy removal of the brownies later.
  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. Stir until the sugars are well combined with the butter. Add the vanilla extract and eggs. Whisk until smooth and well incorporated.
  • In a separate bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, unsweetened cocoa powder, salt, and baking powder (if using). Sifting ensures there are no lumps in the cocoa powder and flour, resulting in a smoother batter.
  • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture and fold them together using a spatula or wooden spoon until fully combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to dense brownies. If you're adding chocolate chips, stir them in at this stage.
  • Pour the brownie batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs (not wet batter).
  • If you like your brownies extra fudgy, check them around the 25-minute mark. For cakier brownies, bake a bit longer.
  • Let the brownies cool in the pan on a wire rack for about 10-15 minutes. Once cooled slightly, cut into squares and serve. Enjoy!
